PsTas Full Wiring Guide

This wires an Unexpected Maker TinyS2 (ESP32-S2) to a DualShock 4 through BOJACK PC817C optocouplers. Driving a TinyS2 pin HIGH turns its optocoupler on, which presses the button. Driving it LOW releases the button.

Each PC817C handles ONE button. Eight buttons means eight chips. There is no way to share a chip between two buttons.

The two ground rails (read this first)

There are two separate "ground" lines in this build, and they must NEVER touch each other. Keeping them apart is the entire reason we use optocouplers.

  • TinyS2 GND rail — the TinyS2's own GND. Every PC817 pin 2 (cathode) and every indicator LED's short leg connect here.
  • DS4 common rail — the controller's own button common. Every PC817 pin 3 (emitter) connects here.
Do NOT connect the TinyS2 GND rail to the DS4 common rail. They stay fully separate. If you join them, you defeat the isolation and can confuse or damage the controller.

PC817C pinout

The notch or dot on the chip marks pin 1. With the notch up, pins go 1-2 down the left and 4-3 down the right.

        +--U--+
  pin 1 |o    | pin 4      pin 1 = anode    (input LED +, from the resistor)
  (A)   |     | (C)        pin 2 = cathode  (input LED -, to TinyS2 GND rail)
        |     |            pin 3 = emitter  (output, to DS4 common rail)
  pin 2 |     | pin 3      pin 4 = collector(output, to the DS4 button pad)
  (K)   +-----+ (E)

Find the DS4 common rail with a multimeter before soldering. With the controller off, one side of every button is usually tied together (the common rail); the other side is that button's own signal pad. The collector (pin 4) goes to the signal pad; the emitter (pin 3) goes to the common rail.
Quick way to find the common rail: in continuity mode, with the controller off, put one probe on the battery negative terminal and touch the other to each button pad. The pad that beeps (reads near zero ohms) is the ground side, which is the common rail. Check two or three buttons. If every button has one pad continuous to battery ground, then battery ground IS the shared emitter rail, and you can run one wire from there to all eight emitters. If no pad connects to battery ground, the buttons are matrix scanned and we need a different approach, so stop and check before soldering.

Optional indicator LEDs

For each button, add a second branch off the same GPIO pin. This is fully independent of the optocoupler and only gives you a light.

   GPIO pin ---[220 ohm]--->|---- LED long leg (anode)
                                  LED short leg (cathode) ----> TinyS2 GND rail

Use the same 220 ohm value. Each pin then sources about 16 mA when pressed, which the TinyS2 handles easily.

Power and isolation

The TinyS2 is powered over its own USB-C cable. The PC817 output side (pins 3 and 4) is powered only by the DS4. The two sides share no wires. Leave the DS4 powered normally over its own USB; we only tap the button contacts.
